Mallapuram

Welcome to Soccer capital

The hilly district of Malappuram falls among the northern districts in the state of Kerala. Closely bordered by the mountain ranges of the Western Ghats on its east, and flanked by the Arabian Sea on the West, Malappuram has an eventful history with the Zamorins of Kozhikode ruling the region for a significant period in the past. The district has also witnessed several local uprisings against the British rule, and was once a prominent center for Hindu-Vedic learning and Islamic philosophy. Historic monuments, natural attractions, ritual art forms, ethnic cuisine and age-old traditions make Malappuram a unique destination for traveler

How to Reach Mallapuram

BY ROAD

Nearest Bus Station Malappuram can be easily accessed by the road. It is well connected by roads to other districts of Kerala. Buses from different nearby states are available to the city.

BY TRAIN

Angadipuram railway station is the nearest railway station to the city. Other railway stattion close to the city are Parappanangadi Railway Station and Tirur Railway Station at a distance of 26 km from the city. Calicut Railway Station is about 50km from the city.

BY AIRLINE

Nearest Airport Calicut International Airport is nearest airport to the city located about 25 km from Malappuram. One can reach Malappuram via this airport and then by road.
